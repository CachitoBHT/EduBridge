Challenges, Journey & Hope

Education Access Isn't Equal — But It Can Be

Not every child grows up with the same opportunities. In some parts of the world — and even within our own country — access to education remains limited by geography, poverty, and social inequality. But numbers only tell part of the story. The human side of education is just as real — and just as urgent.

The Education Gap: A Challenge We Must Address

In rural China, education remains a luxury for many. From limited resources to high student-teacher ratios, children often lack the tools they need to succeed. Without intervention, this gap will only widen — creating generations left behind.

Urban vs Rural School Funding

This pie chart shows the distribution of per-student education funding in China (2025) between urban and rural areas. Urban students receive 58.8% of the funding, while rural students receive only 41.2%. This highlights a clear funding gap, with urban students enjoying significantly more resources. It reflects the ongoing urban-rural inequality in China's education system.

Student-Teacher Ratio (2020-2025)

This bar chart compares the student-teacher ratio in urban and rural China from 2020 to 2025. Rural areas consistently have much higher ratios, from 22 in 2020 to 19.5 in 2025. Urban areas maintain low ratios, dropping from 8 to 6.8, showing better teacher availability. This emphasizes the imbalance in classroom resources between rural and urban schools.

The Journey - My Experience in Eryuan

"Two Months in Eryuan: The Power of Presence."

For two months, I taught English, Math and Art at Eryuan Primary School in Dali. The classrooms were modest, but the children were bright and full of potential. With just a few resources and a lot of heart, I witnessed transformations — in them and in myself.

Classroom Transformation
+92% Engagement 45 Days 28 Students

When I first arrived, the classroom had sparse lighting, worn-out desks, and broken chalkboards that created a difficult learning atmosphere. With the help of small community donations, we were able to transform the space into a welcoming environment with vibrant educational posters and brighter lighting that immediately lifted the children's spirits.

Confidence Growth
+85% Participation 60 Days 32 Students

The children initially hesitated to participate, afraid to make mistakes in front of their peers. Through daily encouragement, positive reinforcement, and interactive group activities, I watched them blossom into confident learners. By the end of my time there, their eager smiles and raised hands showed how much they'd grown.

Uniform Pride
100% Uniformity 2 Weeks 32 Students

One of the most visible changes came with the introduction of new school uniforms. Where students once wore mismatched or worn-out clothes, they now had a shared identity that fostered unity and pride. The joy in their eyes when they first put on the uniforms was unforgettable - they finally felt seen and equal to their peers.

Campus Revival
+120% Activity 3 Weeks 32 Students

The school yard was originally a barren space with no color or equipment. After organizing a community painting day and securing donated play equipment, we transformed it into a vibrant space for play and learning. Watching the children run, laugh, and explore their new environment was one of my most rewarding experiences.

The Hope - How We Can Make a Difference

"Building Hope, One Act at a Time"

Positive change is possible. Whether through volunteering, donating, or spreading awareness, every action counts. With collective effort, we can create environments where every child has the chance to dream, learn, and thrive.

China: Jack Ma Rural Teachers Program

“Jack Ma: Rural Education Integral to China's Future”

Since its launch in 2015, the Jack Ma Rural Teachers Program has selected 100 outstanding rural teachers annually, awarding each ¥100,000 and offering a multi-year training plan. In 2017 alone, the foundation pledged over ¥300 million RMB to bolster rural teaching capacity. Over a decade, the initiative has built sustainable teacher networks and dormitories that allow educators to stay and serve communities long-term.

By 2025, hundreds of teachers across remote regions have benefited. Many report feeling seen, supported, and motivated. As Mr. Liu from Guizhou shared: “For the first time, I felt that being a rural teacher matters to society.”

“This support gave me both confidence and the means to build something lasting in my hometown.”
— Mr. Liu, Guizhou

The results are clear: long-term investment in teachers leads to lasting community impact.

Global: Room to Read - Girls' Education Program

“Fatima from Nepal, first in her village to graduate high school.”

Room to Read has impacted over 50 million children in 28 countries, surpassing its 2025 goal. More than 3.7 million girls have been supported by the Girls' Education Program, with a 96% retention rate and 92% receiving life-skills curriculum ([source](https://roomtoread.org/impact-and-reach/)) :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

Their work focuses on four key pillars: mentorship, family engagement, scholarships, and safe learning environments. Studies show that enrolling girls in secondary school increases their future earnings by 10-20% per additional year of education, and can reduce infant mortality rates significantly ([source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socioeconomic_impact_of_female_education)) :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}.

“With their support, I was the first girl in my village to finish high school.”
— Fatima, 18, Nepal

This program highlights how holistic, data-driven education transforms lives, communities, and gender norms one empowered girl at a time.
